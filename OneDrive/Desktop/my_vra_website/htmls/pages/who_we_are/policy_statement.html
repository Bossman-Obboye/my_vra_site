Privacy Policy

Your privacy is paramount. This policy outlines how the Volta River Authority collects, uses, protects, and discloses your personal information.

Our Commitment to Your Privacy

The Volta River Authority (VRA) is committed to protecting the privacy and security of the personal data we collect and process. This Privacy Policy explains our practices regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of your information through our various services and interactions, in compliance with the Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) of Ghana and other applicable laws.

By interacting with VRA, you consent to the practices described in this policy. We encourage you to read this Privacy Policy carefully to understand how we handle your personal data.

Information We Collect

We collect various types of information to provide and improve our services, manage our operations, and fulfill our legal and regulatory obligations. The information we collect falls into two main categories:

1. Personal Data

This refers to information that can be used to identify you directly or indirectly. Depending on your interaction with VRA (e.g., as a customer, employee, partner, or website visitor), this may include:

  • Contact Information: Name, address, email address, phone number.
  • Identification Information: National ID, passport details, date of birth.
  • Financial Information: Bank account details for payments or billing, credit history (where relevant for service provision).
  • Utility Service Data: Meter numbers, consumption data (electricity, water where applicable), service history, billing records.
  • Employment Information: For job applicants or employees, this includes resumes, work history, educational background, and payroll information.
  • Communications Data: Records of your interactions with us, including calls, emails, and feedback.

2. Non-Personal Data (Usage Data)

This includes information that cannot be used to identify you personally but is related to your use of our website and services. This may include:

  • Device Information: IP address, browser type, operating system.
  • Usage Patterns: Pages visited, time spent on pages, navigation paths, referring websites.
  • Aggregated Data: Statistical or demographic data which we may derive from your personal data but which is anonymized and cannot identify you.

How We Use Your Information

VRA uses the collected information for various purposes, primarily to fulfill our mandate and provide efficient services:

  • Service Provision: To provide, operate, maintain, and manage electricity generation and transmission services.
  • Billing and Payments: To process payments, manage billing cycles, and provide financial statements.
  • Customer Support: To respond to inquiries, complaints, and service requests.
  • Operations Management: To monitor and optimize our infrastructure, manage outages, and ensure reliability.
  • Safety and Security: To maintain the security of our operations, facilities, and systems, and to prevent fraud.
  • Compliance and Legal Obligations: To comply with legal, regulatory, and audit requirements (e.g., Data Protection Act 2012, energy sector regulations).
  • Internal Business Purposes: For data analysis, research, auditing, developing new services, improving our website, and identifying usage trends.
  • Communication: To send you service announcements, administrative messages, updates, and other relevant information.
  • Employment and HR: For recruitment, payroll, employee benefits, and managing employee relations.

We process your personal data based on your consent, to fulfill contractual obligations, for compliance with legal obligations, or where we have a legitimate interest to do so.

How We Share Your Information

VRA may share your information with third parties only when necessary and in accordance with this Privacy Policy and applicable laws. We do not sell your personal data to third parties.

  • Service Providers: We may share data with third-party service providers who perform services on our behalf (e.g., IT support, payment processing, maintenance services, consulting). These providers are contractually obligated to protect your data and use it only for the purposes for which it was provided.
  • Regulatory Bodies and Government Agencies: We may disclose information to comply with legal or regulatory obligations, court orders, or government requests (e.g., Data Protection Commission, Energy Commission).
  • Affiliates and Subsidiaries: Information may be shared with VRA's subsidiary companies or affiliates for operational purposes, consistent with this policy.
  • Legal Requirements and Protection of Rights: We may disclose your information if we believe it's necessary to investigate, prevent, or take action regarding illegal activities, suspected fraud, situations involving potential threats to the physical safety of any person, or as evidence in litigation.
  • With Your Consent: We may share your information with other parties when we have your explicit consent to do so.

Data Security

VRA implements robust technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal data from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption: Using encryption for data transmission where appropriate.
  • Access Controls: Restricting access to personal data to authorized personnel only.
  • Physical Security: Protecting our facilities and equipment from unauthorized physical access.
  • Regular Audits and Monitoring: Continuously monitoring our systems for vulnerabilities and potential security breaches.
  • Employee Training: Ensuring our employees are trained on data protection best practices and policies.

While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal data, no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure. Therefore, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. When your personal data is no longer required, we will securely delete or anonymize it.

Factors that influence our retention periods include:

  • The duration of our relationship with you and the services we provide.
  • Legal obligations that require us to retain data for specific periods.
  • The necessity for fraud prevention and detection.
  • The need to resolve disputes or enforce our agreements.

Your Data Protection Rights

Under the Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843), you have certain rights concerning your personal data. These rights may include:

  1. The Right to Be Informed: You have the right to be informed about the collection and use of your personal data.
  2. The Right to Access: You have the right to request access to your personal data that we hold.
  3. The Right to Rectification: You have the right to request that VRA correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  4. The Right to Object to Processing: You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.
  5. The Right to Prevent Processing for Direct Marketing: You have the right to prevent the processing of your personal data for direct marketing purposes.
  6. The Right to Prevent Processing Causing Damage or Distress: You have the right to object to processing that causes or is likely to cause unwarranted substantial damage or distress.
  7. The Right to Prevent Automated Decision Making: You have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing.
  8. The Right to Compensation: If you suffer damage by reason of contravention of any provision of Act 843, you may be entitled to compensation.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details provided in the "Contact Us" section below. We may need to request specific information from you to help us confirm your identity and ensure your right to access your personal data (or to exercise any of your other rights).

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website may use "cookies" and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience, analyze site usage, and improve our services. Cookies are small data files stored on your device.

  • Strictly Necessary Cookies: Essential for the website to function correctly.
  • Analytical/Performance Cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting information anonymously.
  • Functionality Cookies: Used to remember your preferences and provide enhanced, more personalized features.

You can manage your cookie preferences through your browser settings. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website.

Links to Other Websites

Our website may contain links to other websites that are not operated by VRA. If you click on a third-party link, you will be directed to that third party's site. We strongly advise you to review the Privacy Policy of every site you visit. We have no control over and assume no responsibility for the content, privacy policies, or practices of any third-party sites or services.

Children's Privacy

Our services are not intended for individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from anyone under the age of 18. If you are a parent or guardian and you are aware that your child has provided us with personal data, please contact us. If we become aware that we have collected personal data from children without verification of parental consent, we will take steps to remove that information from our servers.
