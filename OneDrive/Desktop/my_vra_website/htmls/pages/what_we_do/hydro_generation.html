Hydro Generation

Harnessing the power of water to provide clean, reliable, and sustainable energy for Ghana's development.

The Backbone of Ghana's Power Supply

Hydroelectric power remains the cornerstone of the Volta River Authority's generation portfolio. Utilizing the vast resources of the Volta River, our hydro plants provide a significant portion of Ghana's electricity, contributing to energy security, economic growth, and environmental sustainability.

Akosombo Hydro Plant

Ghana's Energy Landmark

The Akosombo Hydroelectric Power Plant, located on the Volta River, is a monumental engineering achievement and a symbol of Ghana's post-independence development. Commissioned in 1965, it is the largest hydroelectric power plant in Ghana and has been instrumental in providing reliable and affordable electricity to the nation and the West African sub-region.

The plant's operations are crucial for managing the Volta Lake, which serves multiple purposes including inland transportation, fishing, and irrigation.

  • Installed Capacity: 1,020 MW
  • Commissioned: 1965
  • Type: Hydroelectric
  • Key Feature: Powers the national grid and contributes to regional energy stability.

Kpong Hydro Plant

Optimizing the Volta River System

The Kpong Hydroelectric Power Plant, commissioned in 1981, is strategically located downstream from the Akosombo Dam. It plays a vital role in optimizing the power generation from the Volta River system by utilizing the regulated outflow from Akosombo. Kpong enhances the overall efficiency and reliability of VRA's hydroelectric operations.

Its design allows for efficient power generation while contributing to the management of water resources in the lower Volta Basin.

  • Installed Capacity: 160 MW
  • Commissioned: 1981
  • Type: Hydroelectric
  • Key Feature: Enhances efficiency of the Volta River power system.
