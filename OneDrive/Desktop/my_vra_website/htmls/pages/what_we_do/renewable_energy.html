Embracing a Sustainable Energy Future

The Volta River Authority is at the forefront of Ghana's renewable energy transition. Recognizing the imperative for climate action and diversified energy sources, we are actively investing in solar and exploring other clean technologies to complement our hydropower and thermal generation, ensuring a resilient and environmentally friendly power supply for the nation.

Our Solar Power Plants

Harnessing the abundant sunshine of Ghana to generate clean electricity.

☀️

Navrongo Solar Power Plant

Located in the Upper East Region, the Navrongo Solar Power Plant was VRA's first utility-scale solar facility. It represents a significant step in diversifying Ghana's energy mix and demonstrates the viability of solar PV technology in the region.

  • Installed Capacity: 2.5 MW
  • Location: Navrongo, Upper East Region
  • Type: Photovoltaic (PV)
☀️

Lawra Solar Power Plant

The Lawra Solar Power Plant, situated in the Upper West Region, further expands VRA's solar footprint. This plant enhances grid stability and provides clean energy to local communities, contributing to regional development.

  • Installed Capacity: 6.5 MW
  • Location: Lawra, Upper West Region
  • Type: Photovoltaic (PV)
☀️

Kaleo Solar Power Plant

The Kaleo Solar Power Plant, developed in phases (A and B), is another key solar asset in the Upper West Region. These plants collectively contribute a substantial amount of renewable energy to the national grid, reinforcing VRA's commitment to clean power.

  • Installed Capacity: 28 MW (Phase A: 13 MW, Phase B: 15 MW)
  • Location: Kaleo, Upper West Region
  • Type: Photovoltaic (PV)

Exploring the potential of wind power to further diversify Ghana's renewable energy portfolio.

Future of Wind Power in Ghana

While VRA's current operational renewable capacity is predominantly solar, we are actively exploring the feasibility and potential of wind energy projects across Ghana's coastal and northern regions. Wind power offers a complementary renewable source that can contribute to energy security and reduce reliance on fossil fuels.

Feasibility studies and resource assessments are ongoing to identify optimal sites and develop strategies for integrating large-scale wind farms into the national grid, aligning with Ghana's long-term renewable energy targets.

  • Status: Under exploration and feasibility assessment.
  • Potential Areas: Coastal regions, Northern Ghana.
  • Goal: Diversify renewable energy mix and enhance grid stability.

Future Outlook for Renewables

VRA is committed to expanding its renewable energy footprint significantly in the coming years. Our strategy involves continued investment in solar PV, exploring new wind energy projects, and researching other emerging clean technologies. This expansion is crucial for meeting Ghana's growing energy demand sustainably and contributing to global climate action.

We aim to integrate more variable renewable energy sources seamlessly into the national grid, leveraging smart grid technologies and energy storage solutions to ensure reliability. Our focus remains on providing clean, affordable, and sustainable power for all Ghanaians.

  • Expansion Plans: Continued growth in solar, potential for wind.
  • Technological Integration: Focus on smart grids and energy storage.
  • National Impact: Contributing to Ghana's climate goals and energy independence.
