Non-Power Activities

Beyond electricity, VRA's commitment to community development, public health, and environmental stewardship in our operational areas.

Our Broader Impact: Beyond Energy

The Volta River Authority's mandate extends beyond power generation to encompass significant social and environmental responsibilities. We actively engage in a range of non-power activities that contribute to the well-being of our host communities and the sustainable management of natural resources, reflecting our commitment to being a responsible corporate citizen.

Our Community & Environmental Initiatives

Exploring the diverse ways VRA contributes to societal development and environmental protection.

🏙️

Local Authority & Township Management

In accordance with Executive Instrument (EI) 42, 1989, VRA administers Local Authority functions in Akosombo Township. We also manage and provide property, facility management, property development, and security services to the Akosombo Township and the VRA community (Township) at Akuse, ensuring well-maintained and secure environments for residents.

  • Scope: Akosombo Township, Akuse Township.
  • Services: Local governance, property management, security.
🏥📚

Education & Health Services

VRA is committed to supporting human capital development and public health. The Authority continues to manage its first and second cycle schools located at Aboadze, Akosombo, and Akuse. We also provide for the health needs of staff, their dependents, and local communities within our operational areas, including Akosombo, Aboadze, Akuse, and Accra.

  • Education: Management of schools in key operational areas.
  • Health: Provision of healthcare services to staff, dependents, and communities.
🦟💉

Malaria Control & Public Health

In a significant public health effort, VRA has signed a Memorandum of Understanding with the Asuogyaman District Health Management Team (DHMT) to provide services to communities along the Volta Lake in the fight against Malaria, Bilharzia, and other water-borne diseases. Additionally, with assistance from the John Hopkins University Center for Communication, VRA has developed a comprehensive strategy document for malaria control, aiming to create a malaria-free environment for its workforce, their families, and surrounding communities.

  • Partnerships: Collaboration with Asuogyaman DHMT, John Hopkins University.
  • Focus: Malaria, Bilharzia, water-borne diseases.
🌳🌱

Environmental Restoration & Afforestation

VRA actively engages in environmental protection initiatives. This includes maintaining a dredging programme at the estuary of the Volta River at Ada to reduce the incidence of Bilharzia and restore the ecosystem in the area. Furthermore, VRA runs extensive afforestation programmes aimed at reducing siltation of the Volta Lake through the restoration of permanent vegetative cover on the slopes bordering the Lake, ensuring the long-term health of this vital resource.

  • Dredging: At Volta River estuary (Ada) for ecosystem restoration.
  • Afforestation: Programmes to reduce siltation of Volta Lake.
