Thermal Generation

Ensuring Ghana's energy security and reliability through efficient and flexible thermal power generation.

Complementing Hydropower for Grid Stability

Thermal power plants play a critical role in VRA's generation mix, providing essential base load, peaking power, and grid stability, especially during periods of low hydro levels or increased demand. Our thermal facilities leverage various fuel types to ensure a robust and flexible energy supply for Ghana's growing economy.

Our Thermal Power Plants

Explore the key thermal power stations contributing to Ghana's energy resilience.

Takoradi Thermal Power Station (TTPS)

Located in Aboadze, Takoradi, TTPS is a cornerstone of Ghana's thermal generation. It comprises multiple units (including TAPCO and TICO) and is vital for providing consistent power to the national grid, particularly to the western corridor and industrial areas.

  • Installed Capacity: ~670 MW (TAPCO T1: 330 MW, TICO T2: 340 MW)
  • Type: Thermal (Combined Cycle & Open Cycle)
  • Fuel Type: Natural Gas / Light Crude Oil (LCO)

Tema Thermal Power Complex (TTPC)

The Tema Thermal Power Complex encompasses multiple plants (including Tema Thermal 1 Plant and Tema Thermal 2 Plant) designed to serve the industrial and residential needs of the Greater Accra region. This complex provides critical support to grid stability and energy supply in the eastern part of Ghana.

  • Installed Capacity: ~190 MW (TT1PP: 110 MW, TT2PP: 80 MW)
  • Type: Thermal (Open Cycle Gas Turbines)
  • Fuel Type: Natural Gas

Takoradi 3 (T3) Thermal Plant

T3 is an advanced combined cycle power plant, also located in Aboadze, designed for high efficiency and operational flexibility. It significantly enhances VRA's capacity to meet peak demand and ensures a more reliable and cost-effective power supply to the national grid.

  • Installed Capacity: 132 MW
  • Type: Thermal (Combined Cycle)
  • Fuel Type: Natural Gas / Light Crude Oil (LCO)

Kpone Thermal Power Station (KTPS)

Situated in Kpone, near Tema, KTPS is a modern thermal power plant that plays a crucial role in strengthening the power supply to the Greater Accra region. Its strategic location and dual-fuel capability contribute significantly to grid stability and energy security.

  • Installed Capacity: 220 MW
  • Type: Thermal (Combined Cycle)
  • Fuel Type: Natural Gas / Diesel

Strategic Importance of Thermal Generation

Thermal power plants are indispensable for maintaining a stable and reliable electricity supply in Ghana. They provide a crucial backup to hydropower, especially during dry seasons when water levels in the Volta Lake are low. Their ability to start up quickly and adjust output makes them ideal for balancing the grid and responding to fluctuations in demand.

Furthermore, the strategic distribution of these plants across different regions enhances grid resilience and minimizes transmission losses. VRA continues to invest in modernizing and expanding its thermal fleet to ensure Ghana's energy future is secure and sustainable.

  • Grid Stability: Provides essential backup and balancing power.
  • Energy Security: Diversifies the generation mix, reducing reliance on single sources.
  • Demand Response: Quickly adjusts output to meet fluctuating electricity demand.
  • Regional Supply: Strategically located to serve key industrial and population centers.
